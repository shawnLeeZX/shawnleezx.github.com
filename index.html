General Interests

I hope that someday, I could combine the following interests to hack the real physical world.

Hacking

I loving building things. Looking back in history, the interaction between philosophers and artisans were a key element to give European society the dynamics Renaissance and Industrial Revolution needed. Its modern spiritual incarnation in our time is called hacking. Partly thanks to the cyber-culture born out of counter culture in 1960s and beyond, the world does not become a Dystopian one. Consequently, I spare considerably time on programming. I am an enthusiastic Linux, Emacs, Vim user. I empathize with the free software philosophy. I am writing a neural network package (in shadow mode still) based on Tensorflow. I am thinking how could we decentralize information and computing power again.

Mathematics

Mathematics is the art to abstract structure from the physical world. I am mostly interested in the mathematical structure underlying neural networks. I have read (some have not understood) all the papers in the area. Philosophically, I think the structure may relate to manifolds. A hidden node in the neural network is the representation of a concept, whose mathematical structure is a manifold, though we still need to come up with its definition. We may need ideas from information geometry, lie group, or perhaps more.

Entrepreneurship

I am also interested in how Machine Learning could be converted to technology that empowers people. Besides intellectual and philosophical curiosity, the impact of Machine Learning as a technology may have on civilization is my major motivation to work in this area. I have some rough ideas, and may start to work on some prototype in the near future. I have some experience in product design. In 2014, collaborating with students of USTC and Stanford University (SU), we designed an arm-mounted haptic navigation wearable device in Design and Innovation Course (ME310) of SU and our product was ranked the best five out of sixty teams in the China-US Maker Contest.
