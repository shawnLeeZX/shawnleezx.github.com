Motivation Sketch

The following are key points that motivate mathematicians to construct a new number theory that is called real number system.

  1. There are numbers that rational number cannot incorporate. In other word, there is gaps in rational number line.
  2. It may be surprising that for some $E \subset Q$, you cannot find the extreme point of $E$ in $Q$. This point is somehow a rephrase of the previous point – there are gaps and least upper bound may residue in the gap.

Therefore, to make the number line to be without gaps, in our daily word, continuous, the real number system is constructed.

Folk Story

For most practical purposes in our daily life, the Field of Rational can be said to be more than adequate as a number system. After all, when we take a measurement with our ruler, we can read at best to the markings on the ruler and those markings are simply fractions of the length of the ruler. Even when we use an electronic calculator and obtained a result whose figures covered the whole screen, we simply round off to our desired accuracy with not much complaint. Now, when we round off something, we are simply getting a result that can be expressed as a fraction. So what exactly is wrong with the Field of Rational?

Most people’s idealization of the real numbers is that they are markings on an imaginary number line. This number line is a sort of conceptual ruler that mathematicians can use to measure their abstract objects exactly. This means that the number line will always give us the exact value, and not just any approximated value, of any quantity we are measuring. Of course, some sort of standardization of the division mark should be used so that everybody’s absolute value agree but that is beside the point. Now, if the Field of Rational gives us such an ideal number system, then we should be able to cover the whole of the number line with rational numbers. If this cannot be achieved, then there will be at least one point on the number line that have no markings, and what value are we going to assign an object whose length happen to hit that mark?

least-upper-bound property

Before we construct the real number system, we first define some relevant definition.

Definition Suppose $S$ is an ordered set, and $E \subset S$. If there exists a $\beta \in S$ such that $x \leq \beta$ for every $x \in E$, we say that $E$ is bounded above, and call $\beta$ an upper bound of $E$.

Lower bound is defined in the same way.

Definition Suppose $S$ is an ordered set, $E \subset S$, and $E$ is bounded above. Suppose there exists an $\alpha \in S$ with the following properties:

  1. $\alpha$ is an upper bound of $E$.
  2. If $\gamma < \alpha$ then $\gamma$ is not an upper bound of $E$.

Then $\alpha$ is called the least upper bound of $E$ or supremum of $E$, and we write

The greatest lower bound or infimum, of a set $E$ which is bounded below is defined in the same manner, denoted as $\alpha = inf E$.

Definition An ordered set $S$ is said to have the least-upper-bound property if the following is true:

If $E \subset S$, $E$ is not empty, and $E$ is bounded above, then $sup E$ exists in $S$.

Real Number System

Theorem There exists an ordered field $R$ which has the least-upper-bound property.

The members of $R$ is are called real numbers.

Now we stop for a moment and to reiterate the understanding one should possess when reaching this point. The gaps in rational number line motivate mathematicians to construct real number system $R$, and the most essential traits which makes $R$ different from $Q$ is that $R$ has least-upper-bound property, which means it has its extreme in its own.

So, how could we construct such a system, called real number system by us, that fulfills least-upper-bound property?

The intuition of two approaches will sketched. The first one is Cantor’s approach and the second one is Dedekind’s approach. Cantor’s approach is described first since it is easier for me to understanding when learning.
