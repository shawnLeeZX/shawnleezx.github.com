Notes about LaTeX.

Multiline Equations

You can present equations with several lines, using the array statement. Inside its declaration you must :

  • Define the number of columns
  • Define column alignment
  • Define column indentation
  • Indicate column separator with & symbol &

Example: {rcl} means: 3 columns with indentations respectively right, center and left.

Multiline Equations Without Equation Number

To use it without equation number, use displaymath environment.

\begin{displaymath}
	\begin{array}{rcl}
	z & = & a \\ f(x,y,z) & = & x + y + z
	\end{array}
\end{displaymath}

Multiline Equations with Equation Number

If you want equation number, use equation environment.

You have two choice, use eqnarray or change displaymath to equation.

eqnarray is a quick way to write three columns eqations, like this:

\begin{eqnarray}
	z & = & a \\ f(x,y,z) & = & x + y + z
\end{eqnarray}

If you want more customized feature, use equation instead:

\begin{equation}
	\begin{array}{rcl}
	z & = & a \\ f(x,y,z) & = & x + y + z
	\end{array}
\end{equation}
