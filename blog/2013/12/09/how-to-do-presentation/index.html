How to do presentation

Dec 9, 2013

Just got some illuminating advice about doing presentation. They come from the teaching team of ME310 course of Stanford University. Share it here.

Will provide more after as I learn more.

  1. Virtually all photos are ambiguous on first sight by persons not previously involved with your project. THEREFORE, all photos are made more informative when annotated with accents, labels, call-outs, whatever. This also applies to sketches and CAD images.

  2. Avoid complex (multi-part) images. Focus on one-message per slide. This often translates to one graphic, table, diagram, or photo per slide. If it is worth showing, it is worth making it intelligible with a minimum of time and attention by the audience.

  3. SHOW ME, donâ€™t tell me. Use images rather than words. Follow the ancient dictum to show, not tell.

  4. Walk the audience through the flow of the diagram, image, visualization if the image is a relatively complex (more than 3 elements).

  5. Prioritize, what are the 1-3 most important things you want the audience to remember. Craft your narrative around these KEY points.

  6. Simplify/Clarify, make each slide as clear, unambiguous and memorable as possible. Delete uninformative graphics, backgrounds, and logos. Make everything READABLE from anywhere in the audience given the presentation technology (that you have tested).
