Playbook for Strategic Foresight and Innovation Reading Notes Series -- Chapter Four

Dec 9, 2013

The perspective chapter is about knowing what has been done, what works and what not, and why some works and some not.

This chapter stops discovering the past but focus on the future. What opportunities lie on in the future.

The overall idea of this chapter is clear. Three methods are provided.

Generational Arcs

Generational Arcs try to catch the big picture of the target people – the age versus population distribution. This is a rough mathematical model to estimate the current state and how will the state shift in the future.

From the modeling result, we may get a sense of the amount of our target peole and how it would change in the future.

Future User

This method have finer granunity. It tries to summarize the typical characteritics and the change may happen in the future of one group of people in the target population – there may be more than one persona to represent different groups in the Generational Arcs.

From the characteritics found, we could have a more concrete person, in other word, persona, to work on.

Future Telling

And future telling focus not on the characteritics but on the scenario. In what scenario would the user in now and in the future?

This is like the procedural programming. You first go the program in your mind once before getting it coded. But the Future Telling should be more physical – a mock scenario is expected to be done.

From the insight found from the mock scenario, what the product will be like would become more concrete.
