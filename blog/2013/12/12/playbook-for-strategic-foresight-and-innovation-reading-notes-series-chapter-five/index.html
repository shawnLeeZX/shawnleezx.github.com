Playbook for Strategic Foresight and Innovation Reading Notes Series -- Chapter Five

Dec 12, 2013

This chapter is about connecting your idea generated from previous work to market, to reality, through which you find lingering assumptions, critical areas untouched and converge your ideas.

Four methods are provided.

White Spots

White spots means unexplored market space. In my view, the point of this method is to give you a start point – find two most critical demision of you context map, draw a 2X2 matrix based on them, fill what the market already have, what are expected to have and what are imagined to have but cannot. Then you find the missing point of the market.

The process gives a hint to begin.

Paper Mockup

This one is similar to the future telling method. However, the latter focus on the function you want to have by testing in senario with imagination. This one is more about bring a rought physical prototype with the function you want by the fastest way.

To try it on real scenario, on real people, you get the realization of your concept and feedback about your idea.

Some nice questions prompt thoughts:

  • Which aspects of your prototype did the audience focus on? Why?

  • Which elements of your idea did your team leave out of your prototype? Why?

  • Which part of the prototype exist today, and which parts need to be built? Which pieces cannot exist today?

  • How should you document and capture the lessons from building and presenting this prototype?

Some advice:

  • Deliberately deep your design rough, so that it looks incomplete and thus invites constructive feedback from others.

Dark horse prototype

At the phase of generating your team’s ideas, there are ones being discarded. However, it happens that the greatest ideas are among the ones that are discarded. So find one of them and do a prototype could give your a valuable lesson.

Change Path

This part is about planning – to find a start point to get you moved and to identify the milestones in the process of achieving your idea.

Simply knowing what to do tomorrow helps break inertia. Ask yourself, what am I going to do on Monday? Who am I going to call?

Stop

This series stops here. There are still two chapters left but they are less relevant to what I am doing right now, so I decide not to read them for the time being.
