Playbook for Strategic Foresight and Innovation Reading Notes Series -- Chapter Three, Perspective

Dec 8, 2013

This chapter is about knowing the background of your research topic. There are some inner laws in the process of design. This chapter provides a clear definition between different phases of doing background reseach, or in other word, benchmarking.

Three methods are provided.

Context Map

Capture what plays an important role in the problem or idea you want to deal with. It emphasizes on the quantity of the factors influencing the problem. It says eight factor, no more to make the problem too complex or no less to capture enough dimensions.

Some notes about drawing the context map. Essentially, it should be no difference from listing a list of factors. However, it is the graph structure that matters. So it would be helpful to make sure the map is big enough for taking notes for the team members.

Progression Curves & Janus Cones

The two methods are all about get the backgound of your problem from a perspective involving time.

At first, the difference between Progression Curves and Janus Cones confuses me.

Context Map emphasizes on factors. This method emphasizes on efforts already put in the problem, the pattern of events happened, and how these events have led to its current state.

But, Janus Cones also emphasizes on events. The book says, Janus Cones is to discern a possible pattern of relationships among many related(and even unrelated) events. Progression Curves also talks about events.

From the words talking about Progression Curves, “you can draw a progression curve for each demision of your context map”, I think Janus Cones is the combination of a bunble of Progression Curves, and it emphasizes on the relation between event that are in different progression curves.

Some comments

In my view, those methods are also very useful for the academic research life I am going to pursue. Hope could try it out in the future.
