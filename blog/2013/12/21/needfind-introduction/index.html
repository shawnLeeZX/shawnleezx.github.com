Needfinding Introduction

Dec 21, 2013

Reading note of a Needfinding book, which I cannot find its author.

Introduction

Leaders in any organization were those people who found important new problems to work on, not necessarily those who ultimately solved the problems. The greatest impact that designers could have on product development, then, was at the earliest stages of product definition.

Note: Find what to solve is always more important than solve the specific problem. In a technical sense, this means you have a big picture on what you are working. This provides you with guidance. But on the other side, you have to have the enough skillset to be able to know how you can solve the problems.

Needfinding is the act of discovering people’s explicit and implicit needs so that designers can create apppropriate solutions.

Needfinding has come to include elements of design planning: providing guidance and strategies for what a successful solution might be.

Needfinding has thus evolved into a methodology that’s a hybrid of both design research and design planning. That hybridity is one of Needfinding’s core strenths.

Question: what does design research and design planning means?

Core Principles of Needfinding

  • Look for needs, not solutions.
  • Make research and design seamless.
  • Spend time in the participants’ world.
  • Look beyond the immediately solvable problem
  • Let the participants set the agenda
  • Collect eclectic forms of data
  • Make findings tangible and prescriptive
  • Iterate to refine the findings.
