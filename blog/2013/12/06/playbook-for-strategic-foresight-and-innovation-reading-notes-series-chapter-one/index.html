Playbook for Strategic Foresight and Innovation Reading Notes Series -- Chapter One

Dec 6, 2013

Introduction

Recently, I am reading a book called Playbook for Strategic Foresight and Innovation. It is easily get lost during reading. I think lots of people finds out. This series of posts are just for recording the ideas generated during the reading process and summarize the structure of the book.

The Foresight Framework

This chapter provides an overview for innovation. Or more specifically, a framework to make your idea into product.

The following chapters in this book are detailed explanations.

Five Planning Phases

There are five planning phases in such framework.

Perspective

See what have been done before in the organization or industry.

This is similar to the benchmarking phase in my Design and Innovation Course.

Opportunity

See what have not been done and what it will be like in the future.

Solution

With your knowledge so far, make prototype for the product you are going to make for the opportunities you found.

Team

Everything nowadays is not a one-man fight.

Vision

What you and your team is going to achieve. Bill Gates’ team was going to let every family has a computer. This is what vision is about.

Two Principles

What you foresee is what you get

There are some similar sayings:

  • Begin with end in mind.
  • Plan what you will do tomorrow today.

You can’t put off tomorrow
