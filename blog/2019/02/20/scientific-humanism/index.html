Scientific Humanism

Feb 20, 2019

While reading the Wikipedia page of Edward O. Wilson, I re-discovered the Humanism Manifesto III.

Previously, I have known the manifesto for several years. Back then at the first time I read the manifesto, it sounded exactly the philosophy that I am looking for. But, the action of Humanist International (HI), the umbrella organization to advocate scientific humanism does not feel right for me. The impression I was left was that HI reactively fights against religion, against non-freedom. Darkness could not be beaten up to bear light; it’s fire that lets lights born. The fight over freedom, over religion has long been won since the capitalism revolution against the theological authoritarianism. The victory was only partly political, which feels like is the focus of HI. The other indispensable part is social-economical. The old feudal agricultural social-economical structure was replaced by a commercial cooperative social-economical structure; and that is the foundation of the political change. However, the problem of our age is exactly the pathological development of barbarism capitalism, which was what socialism revolutionized against in the past, the root of the social tumult in the past century, and the rising of populism recently. That is to say, I feel that HI is a legacy fighting a ghost under the banner of scientific humanism. That’s why I gradually lost my attention on it previously.

But then, I did not know Edward O. Wilson, and his Consilient of Knowledge. I did not know that the first manifesto was drafted under the leadership of John Dewey. That is to say, I wrongly mistook HI for Humanist Manifesto.

Now I started to think that the manifesto is the philosophy of our time in its infancy. I am investigating it from time to time. The investigation mostly focuses on a new social-economical structure could be built on such a philosophy, and what kind of a new institution that would be devised from the new social-economical structure; and also the alignment of force that under such a philosophy — if there exists a community like the Invisible College in the Age of Reason, the Vienna Circle in the pre-WWII age, or Homebrew Computer Club in the aftermath of Counter Culture.

Thematic Development of Western Philosophy Since Renaissance

Updated on May 26th.

I just start to be aware of the full spectrum of philosophy developed under the banner of humanism, which is quite diverse, and overwhelming (for now). The humanist UK has a summary of the philosophers who developed scientific humanism in the 20th centry. I am not familiar with the development outlined there, though I start to understand the progress in philosophy in the 20th, which I previously thought has stalled ever since Satre and Wittgenstein — the political philosophy, e.g., the Theory of Justice by Rawls in my opinion is derivative philosophy, an extension of social liberalism, not a very novel philosophical development.

I would outline an over-simplifying thematic development philosophy since the Renaissance as follows, partly for research logging purpose.

Starting from the 14th century, the commercial development (further research needed) at the end of Middle Age led to the social-economical precondition, and political motivation (to assert the King’s authority instead of the Pope’s), to make possible the development of the wide awareness of human potential. This was the start of the modern humanism. It mostly reflected in arts, and a renewed interests to understand the natural world, instead of settling with superstitious explanation. The philosophy in this period mostly focused on counter-strike the established theology, e.g., the works of Erasmus on Bibles. The corrupted ruling class catalyzed this process, which led to the Protestant movements. The agents of the development in this period is the artists gathered under King’s patronage, and the Protestants.

The crumpling of the worldview build by God and angels led to a reconstruction of a new worldview. First, the worldview was built by seeking the wisdom in Ancient Greek philosophers. Then as time passed, intellectuals started to formulate a systematical rational view of the world. As any novel ideas in its budding period, an over optimistic outlook prevailed, and it led to the under-trained to think that a complete explanation of nature, universe and human society could be achieved through rational thinking. The agents of the development in this period were the natural philosophers, or initial scientists, loosely named the Invisible College. They were mostly aristocrats interested in science. Yet to note that God was never completely disappear in the new philosophy, as in “All men are created equal, that hat they are endowed by their Creator with certain unalienable Rights …”

For about a century, the optimistic outlooks started to disappoint people, especially for the ones that were outsider of the development. The unanswered emotional parts of rationality led to the development to the Romantic Age in the first half of the 19th century. This long suppressed philosophy that looks at the present instead of the future, which reason normally looks at. It let people to treasure the ephemeral now, and focus on living a life, instead of rationally thinking what life is, and could be. Ultimately, it is more livable for the most. Reason represents the ascetic side of humans, while romantics represents the aesthetic side. No one can live in either side of them from the beginning to the end.

However, the emphasis on individuality, and feeling tends to be self-centered, and makes people unaware of the struggling of the others. The development of Romantics accompanied, or co-evolved major political and social-economical development. First, by the 18th century, the common purpose that bound the humanists broke loose: the authoritarianism of theological rule had been overthrown. The dragon slayer became the dragon. Second, the industry revolution started. The original agricultural style exploitation had morphed to another form, the capitalism exploitation.

The world slipped into the imperial period, where major powers fought with each other to get lands that contained raw materials and produced labor, and markets where the manufactured goods can be sold to keep the capitalism flying. This was the darkest time of capitalism. As the dragon slayers turned evil, the new slayers were born, which was socialism. It gave the poor the Utopian hope that the Heaven once had given.

Meanwhile, as liberalism threw away theological authoritarian, it also destroyed moral constraints that were critical to build community, which was critical to exist in the ruling class to build bonds that were beyond material interests. As the lower class turned to socialism, the now ruling liberalism elites tried to instill the morality back in the Victoria Queen’s reign. And during the reign, things were good. (more facts check needed)

Then time passed into the 20th century. The development of socialism could be understood as the further broadening of the individual rights not only to the aristocrats, but also commoners. Its gravest errors were that it takes a considerable personal journey and education to become an aristocrats, in the sense of honor, merits, service etc, not in the sense of birth rights. Without it, it is mob ruling. The Victoria moralism was a look back to the good days of Christianity, thus it could cease to exist as the legendary Queen stepped down.

As discussed, the liberalism society was built on a worldview reconstructed through rationality. Thus, it subjected to change as the worldview had been changed by rationality. A very critical development happened at the turn of the century: a majority of the intellectuals started to realize the so glorified humans are just another species of animals. The realization mostly based on the wide acceptance of the bleak view of Darwin’s works, and the mechanical deterministic physics worldview matured at the turn of the century. In simple words, the long overdue systematic explanation since the Age of Reason actually gave the answer that humans are just meaningless in the cosmetic sense. We are not the beloved children of the God, or the measure of all things.

Thus, the once respected guiding force of rationality broke down. It was also a time of sharp conflict. The cake were not big enough for all nations in the world, especially in the Europe, thus the ruling class clashed with each other. The problem of fetish capitalism described before had developed to the point of uncontainable, thus the commoners clashed with the ruling class. Meanwhile, rationality gave a doomed “Survival of the Fittest” worldview. People turned to nationalism and to communism for hope. War broke out.

Wars started, and wars ended. The wars taught everyone a grave lesson. Nation should respect each other sovereignty, and try to cooperate no matter how insignificant the other part may seem. The privileged should respect the under-privileged, otherwise, no one would live a decent life. The United Nations finally worked, and social liberalism was born. The new world order started to build around a new global liberal order, and nations cooperated with each other on trades. Colonies gradually disappeared. On the pressure of Soviet Union, and the influence of socialism, unions became a major force in the capitalism societies, and counter-balanced the fetish for profits.

However, the philosophical existential crisis for humans never ceased. To live without a cosmetic purpose, philosophers tried to find solid ground men’s lives may build on. One school was the logic school, represented by Bertrand Russell etc., that tried to build a solid foundation of the world through axioms and logic. The other school was the continent school, the phenomenology, represented Satre etc. The logic school failed since the work of Godel, who proved an axiom system cannot prove itself is right, and died more or less as the consequence of such a devastating proof. The phenomenology ended up in “aghast”: men created meanings for themselves, despite the artificial fabrication. Phenomenology identified a rather crucial fact that all knowledge comes from the senses of people. But it did not build a systematic livable philosophy on its own. At the end of the day, Satre turned to Humanism. They both had fallen short because they could not go beyond the ontology set by science, which waited for a new development in 1970s.

Around 1970s, a new kind of science called complexity science was born. It swiped away the deterministic view on the world, and gave an epic instead of cosmetic narrative on humans. We human were evolved to be capable of love, to think rationally, to be empathetic, to be artistic. The progress in modern biology, complex system, nonlinear science has drawn another humanitarian epic narrative, out of the Darwin jungle one — indeed we are stardust, but we are also children of the sun: “Human social existence, unlike animal sociality, is based on the genetic propensity to form long-term contracts that evolve by culture into moral precepts and law. They evolved over tens or hundreds of millennia because they conferred upon the genes prescribing their survival and the opportunity to be represented in future generations. We are not errant children who occasionally sin by disobeying instructions from outside our species. We are adults who have discovered which covenants are necessary for survival, and we have accepted the necessity of securing them by sacred oath.” (Quote Consilience, Wilson, 1999) Though evolved from animals, and possessing their baser instincts, humans are uniquely different from animals. The difference we may call the divine side of humans. Though cosmetically insignificant, we are enough for ourselves; we are enough for our planets; we are enough for our universe. This development gives solid ontological foundation for liberty, morality (fraternity and equality), rationality, and creativity. The philosophy built on such ontology is called humanism now. I will write it as scientific humanism for clarity.

Meanwhile, as the memory of the wars faded away, history is repeating itself again. As technology and expertise had become a more important factor in production than labors, the power balance between worker union and capitalists broke down. Power had become increasingly concentrated on the top 1 percent who are not incentivized care for the rest. The tension accumulated ever since information technology was born, and globalization started, began to erupt after the fire light by the 2008 financial crisis. This time, socialism is of no match, since headcount won’t count this time. The fetish for growth incentivized by capitalism needs fixing. A new incentive mechanism that underpins the society need to be worked out.

Right now, the scientific humanism is merely a philosophy, a view on life, not even a well known one (as for as I know). I tend to think it has the potential to build an alternative incentive mechanism in replace of the capitalism. And I am learning how things have been doing so far now.
